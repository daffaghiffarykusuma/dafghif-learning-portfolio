Mentoring Week 2 - SDG-Voted Project Concept Development Workbook
PurposeStart from shared SDG interest, not from the mentees' original ideas. The group first votes on favorite SDGs, then builds a new project concept from the most-voted SDG base.
Recommended voting ruleEach of the 3 mentees must choose exactly Top 8 favorite SDGs and rank them 1-8. With 17 SDGs, this makes agreement very likely: the chance that at least one SDG is chosen by all 3 is about 90.9%, and at least one SDG gets 2+ votes is effectively 100%.
Why not require unanimity?Because the mentees are diverse, this workbook now prioritizes high overlap. The group should still use ranking points and the reality check so the final SDG base is not just broad agreement, but also projectable in Japan.
Decision ruleUse the highest vote count first. If tied, use priority points from ranking. If still tied, choose the SDG with the clearest current Japan problem and easiest small pilot.
Week 2 focusAfter the base SDG is chosen, develop a project concept: background problem, solution, vision and mission, partners, timeline, budget, target, and task distribution.
Mentor stanceDo not ask whose original idea won. Ask: which SDG do we collectively care about, what problem in Japan is real, and what can 3 mentees realistically build?
Recommended flow
0-10 minExplain that original ideas are paused. The group will restart from SDG interest and shared relevance.
10-25 minEach mentee ranks Top 8 SDGs privately in W1 SDG Voting.
25-40 minReveal vote summary and identify the top SDG or top SDG cluster using vote count and priority points.
40-65 minUse W1 SDG Reality Check to turn top SDGs into Japan-grounded problem options.
65-90 minAgree on one SDG base and one problem direction, then continue into Week 2 concept development.
