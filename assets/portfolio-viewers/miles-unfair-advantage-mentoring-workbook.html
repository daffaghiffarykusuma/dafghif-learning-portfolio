MILES Unfair Advantage Mentoring Workbook
Hybrid format: one quick overview sheet for the mentor and separate asset-level worksheets for each MILES area.
How to use this workbook
1Write one SMART goal for this mentoring cycle.Keep it specific so the student and mentor can measure progress.
2Use the Example Bank only for ideas.Students should rewrite examples in their own words.
3Fill the five MILES sheets, one asset per row.Add Current Stage, Target Stage, Priority, and Impact Weight.
4Complete the MILES Overview.This gives the mentor a fast category-level summary.
5Use the score columns to choose the top 1–3 assets in Focus Plan.Higher scores usually mean the asset deserves attention sooner.
Simple stage scale (Current Stage and Target Stage)
1Not active yet or very hard to use
2Small or inconsistent advantage
3Usable with support
4Strong and reliable
5Signature advantage ready now
Gap Score = Target Stage - Current Stage. Use Priority and Impact Weight to decide what to improve first.
