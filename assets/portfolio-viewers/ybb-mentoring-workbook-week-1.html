2. Week 1 Focus and Ground Rules
Use this sheet to explore ideas first, not to lock in a final proposal yet. • Focus on project ideation and problem identification related to SDGs. • You may use AI tools to help generate, expand, or refine ideas, but make sure the final ideas still reflect your own judgment and the group context. • Since the designated nation is Japan, try to ground your ideas in current local needs, community conditions, and cultural context there. • The group will present only one collaborative project in the end. • No one should impose personal ideas; look for overlap, relevance, and feasibility. • Good Week 1 output: a clear problem worth solving and 1–3 promising project directions.
3. Explore Up to Three Possible Ideas
Idea 1Example answer
Working Idea / ThemeCommunity digital support and social connection for older adults
Related SDG(s)SDG 3 (Good Health and Well-being), SDG 10 (Reduced Inequalities), and SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities and Communities)
Problem Statement (What is the problem?)Japan's aging society means some older adults face isolation and struggle to access digital information, services, and community activities.
Why is this a real problem?Older adults already make up 29.3% of Japan's population, so barriers to connection and access affect many communities.
Who is affected?Older adults living alone, senior community members, and family caregivers.
Where does it happen?Neighborhoods with many elderly residents, community centers, public housing areas, or smaller towns.
When / in what context is it most visible?It becomes visible during daily service access, local events, health appointments, and emergency information sharing.
Current Condition (How does it look now?)Helpful services exist, but many are scattered, digital-first, or not confidence-building for seniors.
Possible Project Direction / ActivityRun a simple intergenerational support program on smartphone basics, local-service navigation, and social meet-ups.
Why I want to explore this ideaIt suits a volunteer team, fits Japan's local context, and can create practical and human-centered impact.
Idea 2Example answer
Working Idea / ThemeSustainable tourism awareness in busy local destinations
Related SDG(s)SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities and Communities), SDG 12 (Responsible Consumption and Production), and SDG 8 (Decent Work and Economic Growth)
Problem Statement (What is the problem?)High visitor numbers can create waste, crowding, and strain on local spaces if tourism is not managed sustainably.
Why is this a real problem?Japan continues to see record inbound tourism, so resident comfort and local sustainability are under pressure in some areas.
Who is affected?Local residents, small businesses, visitors, and cleaning or transport staff.
Where does it happen?Tourist streets, stations, cultural sites, shopping areas, and nearby neighborhoods.
When / in what context is it most visible?It is most visible during holidays, weekends, festivals, and peak travel seasons.
Current Condition (How does it look now?)Multilingual guidance, waste sorting, and respectful visitor behavior are still inconsistent across busy areas.
Possible Project Direction / ActivityCreate a multilingual visitor-awareness campaign with simple etiquette, waste-sorting tips, and local-area guidance.
Why I want to explore this ideaIt connects SDGs with a visible issue in Japan and can be adapted to the team's designated community.
Idea 3Example answer
Working Idea / ThemeCommunity heat safety and everyday disaster readiness
Related SDG(s)SDG 3 (Good Health and Well-being), SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities and Communities), and SDG 13 (Climate Action)
Problem Statement (What is the problem?)Many people are not fully prepared to respond to extreme heat or everyday disaster risks in a clear, practical way.
Why is this a real problem?In Japan, official systems for heat and disaster alerts are important, but some residents still need easier, more practical guidance.
Who is affected?Older adults, school-age youth, foreign residents, families, and other community members.
Where does it happen?Schools, community centers, neighborhoods, and places with many newcomers or elderly residents.
When / in what context is it most visible?It is especially visible in summer heatwaves and when people need to react quickly to local emergency information.
Current Condition (How does it look now?)Official information exists, but people may not practice what to do or understand how to prepare in daily life.
Possible Project Direction / ActivityHold a community workshop on heat safety, emergency kits, evacuation basics, and easy-to-understand local guidance.
Why I want to explore this ideaIt is practical, easy to deliver in small sessions, and highly relevant to living safely in Japan.
