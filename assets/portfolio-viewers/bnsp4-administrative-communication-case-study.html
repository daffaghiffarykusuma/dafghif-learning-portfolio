Case Study

Competency-Based Administrative Communication Learning Program

This case study consolidates the administrative communication portfolio items into one end-to-end learning design case: diagnosis, proposal, lesson plan, facilitator material, and evaluation tools.

Reviewer Context

  • Use case: Review competency-based learning design capability for workplace communication.
  • Scope: Training needs analysis, program proposal, session design, learning material, post-test, reaction form, observation form, and performance rubric.
  • Evidence limit: The artifacts show design quality and measurement structure. They do not independently prove learner outcome impact.

Case Flow

  1. Diagnose the gap: Identify administrative communication needs and translate them into practical training priorities.
  2. Design the program: Convert the need into a competency-based proposal and 36-hour lesson plan with sessions, activities, OJT practice, and evidence requirements.
  3. Build learning support: Prepare facilitator-ready material for confident, professional, and structured workplace communication.
  4. Measure quality: Use post-test, reaction feedback, observation, and rubric tools to evaluate understanding, experience, and behavior transfer.
