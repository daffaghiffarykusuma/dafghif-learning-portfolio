Slide 2

  • THE URGENCY
  • Why Neurodivergent Inclusion Matters in Japan
  • Rising Prevalence
  • Exceeding Global Averages
  • 3.26%
  • ASD prevalence for 2014 birth cohort in Japan, significantly higher than global averages. The burden continues to rise, with projections showing sustained increase through 2050.
  • Mental Health Crisis
  • Suicidal Ideation Risk
  • 56%
  • Lifetime suicidal ideation among young adults with both ASD and ADHD traits. 33.9% across all neurodivergent youth - a silent epidemic requiring urgent intervention.
  • Cultural Barriers
  • The Meiwaku Factor
  • 「迷惑」
  • The cultural value of "not burdening others" prevents neurodivergent individuals from requesting accommodations. Stigma remains significantly higher than in Western contexts even after awareness interventions.
  • Implementation Gap
  • Law vs. Reality
  • 2024
  • Japan's amended law mandating reasonable accommodation took effect April 2024, yet practical implementation remains limited. The disconnect between legal protection and workplace reality creates invisible barriers.

  • Problem Background & Target Beneficiary
  • The Core Problem
  • Neurodivergent individuals in Japan face
  • systemic barriers
  • in education, employment, and social participation due to persistent stigma, lack of public awareness, and inadequate practical accommodations.
  • Despite legal protections under the 2016 Act (amended 2024), the
  • implementation gap
  • between policy and practice leaves many without support. Cultural factors compound these challenges, making Japan's neurodivergent inclusion landscape uniquely complex.

  • Social Life

  • Conditions
  • ASD, ADHD, and related neurodevelopmental conditions
  • Location
  • Urban Japan, primarily Tokyo and surrounding prefectures
  • Focus

  • Key Challenge

Slide 5

  • ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
  • Root Causes & Systemic Barriers
  • Cultural Stigma
  • The proverb
  • "the nail that sticks out gets hammered down"
  • reflects intense conformity pressure. Standing out risks mockery and bullying, especially in classrooms.
  • Impact: Social exclusion
  • Lack of Awareness
  • Studies show autism is
  • significantly more stigmatized in Japan
  • than the US. Even after intervention, Japanese students stigmatize autism at high rates unlike US students.
  • Impact: Underdiagnosis
  • Inadequate Accommodation
  • Despite the
  • 2024 legal mandate
  • , practical implementation remains limited. Many businesses lack knowledge or resources to provide meaningful accommodations.
  • Impact: Workplace barriers
  • Limited Infrastructure
  • Insufficient specialized educational and workplace support services. Existing support focuses on children or severe disabilities, leaving
  • youth and high-functioning adults underserved
  • .
  • Impact: Service gaps
  • Communication Barriers
  • Meiwaku culture
  • makes it difficult to express needs. Lack of constructive dialogue frameworks means many suffer in silence rather than request help.
  • Impact: Silent suffering
  • These interconnected barriers create a cycle:
  • Stigma prevents disclosure → Non-disclosure limits accommodation → Lack of accommodation reinforces stigma
