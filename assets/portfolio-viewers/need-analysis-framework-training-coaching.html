Need Analysis Framework for Initial Client Meetings
Designed for consultants offering training and coaching services across industries. Use it to diagnose the need before proposing any solution.
How to use this workbook1) Start with the Mandatory Core sheet for live meetings. 2) Use the Question Bank to deepen diagnosis where needed. 3) Capture answers in the Client Notes Template. 4) Use the Meeting Flow sheet if you want a structured conversation path.
Meaning of MandatoryAsk these in almost every initial meeting, unless the answer is already known from documents or prior stakeholder input.
Meaning of OptionalUse these to deepen understanding, validate assumptions, or explore context when the issue is complex, high-risk, or politically sensitive.
Applicable ToBoth = relevant for training and coaching conversations. Training = mainly relevant when a group learning intervention is likely. Coaching = mainly relevant when 1:1 or group coaching is being considered.
Good practice in the first meetingStay diagnostic rather than sales-oriented. Test whether training or coaching is actually part of the solution. Separate root causes from symptoms. Distinguish what is urgent from what is important.
Recommended outputs after the meetingA concise problem statement, target audience definition, likely root causes, intervention-fit hypothesis, success measures, constraints, and suggested next diagnostic step.

Suggested Flow for the Initial Discovery Meeting
StepConversation BlockTypical TimePurposeUse These Sheets
1Context and Trigger5–10 minUnderstand the organization, the trigger, and why this matters now.Start Here, Mandatory Core
2Business Need10–15 minClarify the business problem, priority outcomes, and what success would mean.Mandatory Core, Question Bank
3Audience and Current State10–15 minIdentify who is affected, how the issue shows up, and what evidence exists.Mandatory Core, Client Notes Template
4Root Cause and Readiness10–15 minTest whether training or coaching is the right intervention and what could block transfer.Mandatory Core, Question Bank
5Delivery Feasibility and Metrics10–15 minExplore practical constraints, desired format, timing, and measures of success.Mandatory Core, Question Bank
6Close and Next Steps5 minSummarize priorities, note gaps, and agree the most useful next diagnostic step.Mandatory Core
