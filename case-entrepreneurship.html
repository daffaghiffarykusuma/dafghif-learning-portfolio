Case Study

Entrepreneurship Program for 5,000+ SMK Students

In partnership with Rumah Siap Kerja and the Ministry of Education, we designed and ran a multi-city entrepreneurship program that helped vocational students transform ideas into real businesses within six weeks.

Client & Audience

  • Rumah Siap Kerja & Ministry of Education
  • 5,000 vocational students across Indonesia
  • Local mentors and school facilitators

Engagement Scope

  • Program design & curriculum architecture
  • Mentor onboarding & facilitator coaching
  • Measurement, reporting, and iteration

Challenge

Vocational students needed more than theory—they required hands-on experience, community support, and metrics to prove the viability of their ideas.

Disparate starting points

Students came from 23 different vocational majors with minimal exposure to entrepreneurship. We had to create a shared baseline in mindset and skill within days.

Limited facilitation capacity

Teachers and mentors were eager but needed a clear playbook, coaching scripts, and tools to keep teams accountable week-to-week.

Proof of impact for stakeholders

Government partners required transparent evidence on learner progress, business viability, and program ROI for future funding decisions.

Our Approach

We paired design sprints with rigorous facilitation support so students could ideate, test, and sell products within a structured timeframe.

  1. Discover

    Interviewed school leaders, employers, and alumni to map required competencies. Built learner personas and success indicators aligned with national curriculum goals.

  2. Design & Enable

    Created a modular curriculum covering problem discovery, prototyping, marketing, and finance. Delivered mentor onboarding kits, weekly coaching scripts, and feedback loops.

  3. Deliver & Amplify

    Facilitated hybrid workshops, ran marketplace simulations, and tracked sales dashboards. Ended with demo days and reporting packs for government stakeholders.
